Cruise Industry Hall of Fame - Outstanding Retailer
CLIA Cruise Lines International Association


On April 5th, 2014, Uf Tukel and Don Walker were inducted inducted into the CLIA Hall of Fame. They were honored at a gala event during the 10th anniversary cruise3sixty conference and trade show in Fort Lauderdale, FL.

The CLIA Hall of Fame honors those individuals, past and present, whose efforts and accomplishments represent a significant contribution to, and a positive impact on, the cruise industry. Honorees are selected based on open nominations from CLIA's membership, followed by a review by the CLIA Trade Relations Committee.

"During this very special 10th Anniversary of cruise3sixty and as we look to an exciting next decade of growth, it is particularly noteworthy that this year's honorees have all played such significant roles in building and shaping the industry", said Christine Duffy, CLIA's president and CEO. "And, they have all been among CLIA's strongest supporters for many years."

Accessible Travel Advocate Power Partner
Special Needs Group


WMPH Vacations was the first agency to have 100% of its agents complete the Certified Accessible Travel Advocate Program. As a result, WMPH was named SNG Accessible Travel Advocate Power Partner, together. The Special Needs Group Certification Program educates agents on the size, scope and needs of the disabled market segment and on protection offered by the ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act).

The South Florida Business Journal is a newspaper covering general business news, major events in Ft. Lauderdale, Miami, and around the world. With sections on global news and local news, business news and political news, Florida cultural life and newspaper classified ads, the Florida Press Association recognized the South Florida Business Journal as the best weekly newspaper of its size.

Top 5000 Fastest Growing Companies in America
Inc. Magazine


The Inc. 5000 is an annual list of the 5000 fastest-growing private companies in the United States. The Inc. 5000 is ranked according to percentage revenue growth over a four-year period. To qualify, companies must be able to show four full calendar years of sales. Additionally, they had to be U.S.-based, privately held, and independent, not subsidiaries or divisions of other companies as of December 31 of the last year measured. Revenue in the initial year must have been at least $200,000, and revenue in the most recent year must have been at least $2 million.
